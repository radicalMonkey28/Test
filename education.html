The Industry Challenge

One decision, several decision-makers, and a cycle that lasts months.

A student researches courses, but a parent often controls the decision and the budget. An institutional buyer weighs curriculum, accreditation, and cost. Each cares about something different, and each searches for something different — yet most education marketing speaks to only one of them.

Compounding this, enrolment cycles are long and seasonal. A prospect who discovers you in one term may not enrol until the next. Without sustained visibility and structured nurture, interest decays quietly between the first search and the moment of decision.

FAQ
Should we market to students or parents?
Both, separately. They search for different things and respond to different messages — students to outcomes and experience, parents to value, safety, and prospects. Effective campaigns speak to each in their own terms.
How do we handle such a long enrolment cycle?
With sustained visibility and structured nurture. Content and SEO keep you present throughout the research phase; automation keeps enquirers engaged until the decision point rather than letting interest fade.
Which channels work for education?
Search captures active research; social and video build desire and reach students where they are; email nurture carries them across the cycle. The mix depends on your programmes and audience — the audit determines it.
Can you support both institutions and edtech products?
Yes. Institutional enrolment and edtech sales differ in cycle and buyer, but both reward visibility during research and disciplined nurture afterwards.
