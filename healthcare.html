The Industry Challenge

Health content is held to a higher standard — by regulators and by Google.

Healthcare sits squarely in Google's YMYL category — Your Money or Your Life. Content that could affect someone's health is judged against strict standards of expertise, authoritativeness, and trust. Medical claims made without credentials, reviewed by nobody, and published on a site with no institutional signals will not rank, regardless of accuracy.

Then there's regulation. Advertising restrictions, patient privacy rules, and claims scrutiny constrain what you can say and where you can say it. Many campaigns that work in other sectors are simply not permissible here. Healthcare marketing succeeds when clinical credibility and compliance are built in from the start — not bolted on after a campaign is rejected.

FAQ
Why won't our health content rank, even though it's accurate?
Accuracy isn't enough. Google needs to verify expertise: named, credentialed authors, medical review, structured data, and institutional trust signals. Without them, health content is held back regardless of quality. Fixing this lifts the whole site.
Can you advertise healthcare services on Google and Meta?
In most cases yes, but health advertising faces stricter review and some categories require certification or are restricted entirely. We structure campaigns to meet those policies from the start rather than after rejection.
How do you handle patient privacy in marketing?
Carefully. Tracking, remarketing, and testimonials all carry privacy obligations in healthcare. We implement analytics with proper consent management and advise on what can and cannot be used.
Do you write clinical content yourselves?
We produce the content and structure, working with your clinicians for medical accuracy and review — which is also exactly what Google's E-E-A-T standards expect to see.
